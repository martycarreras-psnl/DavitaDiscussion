Live prompts you'll see
Step 1 — Retention Intelligence I'm a clinic manager onboarding a new dialysis patient care technician. Based on healthcare workforce research, what are the top factors that determine whether a frontline healthcare worker stays or leaves within their first 6 months? Rank them by impact and give me specific, measurable cues I can watch for — things I'd actually notice during a shift or a 1:1. Make it practical, not academic.
Step 2 — Personalized Playbook Now turn those retention factors into a week-by-week early warning guide for Jordan's first 6 months. For each phase — first week, first 30 days, 60 days, 90 days, and 6 months — give me: (1) the specific behavioral cues that signal the teammate may be struggling, (2) the empathetic conversation starters I should use if I notice them, and (3) the one high-impact action I can take to course-correct. Format this as a Word document titled "Teammate Retention Playbook — Jordan Rivera" that I can reference during our 1:1s.
Step 3 — Orchestrate Belonging Jordan Rivera starts next Monday. Post a warm welcome message to our Harrisburg East clinic Teams channel — make it personal and mention our Village values. Then schedule three meetings this week: a 30-min orientation with me, a mentor intro with Marcus Johnson, and a team lunch with the full care team. Find times that work for everyone.
Step 4 — Proactive Coaching Set up proactive check-ins for Jordan's first 6 months. Every other Friday, remind me with the specific cues I should be watching for at that stage of their journey — and include the empathetic conversation starters from the playbook. For example, at the 2-week mark, remind me to watch whether Jordan is asking questions or staying silent, and suggest I say something like "I remember my first weeks here — what's been the hardest part so far?" Make each reminder stage-appropriate so it evolves as Jordan grows.
💚 Projected impact:  A 5-point reduction in field attrition = 2,750 teammates retained per year, preserving institutional knowledge and patient relationships.
